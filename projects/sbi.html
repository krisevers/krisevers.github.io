Models are used to describe the world around us. Mechanistic models are a subset of models which can be used to express how natural phenomena are occur mechanistically. Sometimes the phenomena are straightforward to explain. For example, the motion of a ball can be described by Newton's laws of motion. However, in many cases, the phenomena are more complex and the models are more difficult to interpret. Linear models can be easily analysed through decomposition of the system.
