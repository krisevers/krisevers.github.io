The History and Evolution of Cortical Column Models

The history of neuroscience is filled with attempts to define the functional unit of the cortex. Heinrich von Waldeyer-Hartz first proposed the neuron theory in 1891, stating the neuron is the basic unit of the nervous system. Based on the work by Ramón y Cajal, he suggested that neurons are the fundamental building blocks of the nervous system, and that they are connected to each other via synapses. In the 1980s, David Marr proposed a tri-level hypothesis for understanding the brain. He suggested that the brain should be understood at three levels: the computational level, the algorithmic level, and the implementational level. The computational level is concerned with the problem the system is trying to solve, the algorithmic level is concerned with the representations and processes that solve the problem, and the implementational level is concerned with the physical substrate that implements the representations and processes. This tri-level hypothesis has been used to interpret the basic unit of the cortex.
